Stimuli-responsive polymers  ·  Nanomedicine

Polymers that respond — and deliver.

We design stimuli-responsive polymers and nanomedicines that sense their environment and act on it: releasing genes, running enzymatic cascades, and reporting back from inside living systems.

Research

Stimuli-responsive nanogels

Crosslinked polymer networks engineered to swell, collapse, or disassemble on cue — pH, temperature, enzymes, or oxidative stress — so that cargo is held until the target environment unlocks it.

RAFT / ATRPthermoresponsivedegradable
— deliver

CRISPR / Cas9 RNP delivery

Polymeric nanocapsules that protect Cas9–sgRNA ribonucleoproteins and ferry them across mucosal barriers, with a focus on transmucosal gene editing for cystic fibrosis.

RNP encapsulationmucosalgene editing
— react

Chemoenzymatic nanoreactors

Enzyme-loaded nanogels decorated with metal nanoclusters that run cascade reactions in confinement — coupling biocatalysis with controlled polymerization and tunable release.

nanozymescascadesbiocatalysis
— report

Theranostics & digital twins

Sensor-coupled, imaging-active platforms paired with physics-informed models that predict nanocarrier transport — closing the loop between what a material does and what we can measure.
